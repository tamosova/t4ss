Types of tails

«STUB» - the tail often consisting of 2-8 vertebrae, which are shortened and deformed, as if planted on top of each other. Vertebrae are interconnecte motionless, fractures may be felt as bumps. Vertebrae can form a spiral, but they are so tightly connected to each other, that it is impossible to determine the direction of the spiral. Sometimes at the end of the tail can be 1-2 partial mobile vertebrae.

«SPIRAL» - consists of 3 - 15 vertebrae deformed in a way that fractures are connected in a fairly sharp angle, forming a spiral, or «hook». Such a tail may have a different directions, it may be bent on the back and several of his vertebrae can connect to the sacrum very tightly. Connection vertebrae can be flexible or semi-flexible possible combination of mobile and fixed parts, while bobtail can «wobble» how different parts of the tail.

«WHISK» - consists of 5-10 vertebrae fractures connected in blunt angles, connections can me flexible, rigid and their combinations. This tail can visually look quite long, up to 1/3 of the normal tail, and incorrectness are determined only by touch.

«RELEGATED BOBTAIL» - begins as a normal tail, direct and free from defects for a 5-7 vertebrae, at the end of tail several deformed vertebrae form "hook" of any degree of mobility. «Hook» by their nature is often a «spiral».
